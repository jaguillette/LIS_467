Hi, I'm Jeremy Guillette. This is a website that I'm making for LIS 467: Web Development. I'm going to write a bit about myself.

Too Many Jobs

I'm currently working at Simmons College as a Usability Research Assistant, Technology Reference Assistant, and Graduate Assistant. I'm also working as a Library Assistant at Wolbach Library, the library for the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. Maybe someday I'll be able to do more than assist people a lot, but currently that's what I do.

Bicycling

I bike as my primary form of transportation. I started when I moved to Boston, and I haven't stopped. My girlfriend actually introduced me to city biking, and now we bike everywhere.

Things I like about biking:

  • Getting everywhere faster, because traffic doesn't exist for me.
  • Not feeling like I have to go to the gym, since transportation is excercise.
  • Never having to wait for a bus to arrive.

Internet stuff

I've been a pretty internet-based person for a while, starting with StumbleUpon in undergrad, which has now progressed into an addiction to Reddit. I'm not a big fan of social media (if I wanted to talk to people, why would I be online?), but I've found it to be a really helpful way of learning things I wouldn't know otherwise. For example, have you ever heard the story of the the 1904 Olympics Men's Marathon? It's one of the most hilarious Wikipedia articles I've ever read, and I found out about it from Reddit.

Board Games

I'm kind of a board game geek. I played a good amount of Dungeons and Dragons in college, and it seems that complicated board games come with the territory. These days I'm not involved in any D&D campaigns, but I do find time to play stuff like Dixit, Dominion, Fluxx, and Munchkin.
